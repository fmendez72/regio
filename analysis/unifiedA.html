Unified Model: Mixed effects ordinal regression

Overview

A quick summary of the regression results. Two models, one with and one without interaction effects. As well as model comparison to see if they are different.

Lastly, the plots show the interaction effects on the marginal probabilities.

Fully specified model

These are the results of the fully specified unified model. The fact that territorial Level is signficant only in the interaction is not a problem at all -its effect was being masked by the Frames and to a lesser extent by Actor. All the more justification for running an interaction model.

Plots

Here we look at the 3 key variables (Level / Actor / Frame)

What to look for in the plots. Horizontal lines indicate No difference between territorial levels (i.e., National and Regional are nearly on same level). Where lines go up or down indicates differences between territorial leves. Also, check which region of the plot that lines are, in the High probability regaion or the Low probability region.

Note: For the Actor dummy, national/subnational/local is called National, EU (as well as national, and other) is called EU/Other.

A quick interpretation taking each Tone separately:

For negative tone, first of all stories in the Eco frame are very, very unlikely to be negative. Theres not much of a discernable difference in the Eco frame between the territorial levels -although Regional referencing EU/Other is the most unlikely when compared to National level. There are big differences with the Politics frame, and this difference is relates to actor. Regional news sources are more likely to have a negative story referencing to National actors, while this is less likely for National level sources. Regarding EU actors in the negative Politics frame, theres no difference.

For Neutral tone, for the Eco frame the difference between territorial levels is with regard to EU Actos -less likely for regional sources to be referencing these in this neutral category. (the reason why is because they are likely to be in the positive tone, see next plot). In the Politics frame no discernable difference regarding the referencing of EU/other actors appears, although there is a slight difference with regard to the referencing of National actors across levels in the neutral tone.

For Positive tone, the obvious difference is the likelihood of being Positive is very different across the two Frames. Yet there are some important differences between the territorial levels. In the Eco frame, there is no real difference between territorial levels when it comes to referencing National actors only. However, Regional sources are considerably more likely to mention EU/other actors in the positive Eco frame than National sources. In the Politics frame the opposite is the case. Its very unlikely for Politics frames to be positive. Interestingly, regional sources are more unlikely to be referencing National actors in the Positive political frame than national sources, while for referencing EU/other actors theres no difference between the levels in the politics frame.

Annex

Check the effect of a storys Word length

Just to check the word length plot. Basically, short stories are more likely to have a positive tone, and less likely to be negative. The opposite is the case for longer stories, less likely to positive and more likely to be negative than medium and and shorter stories.
